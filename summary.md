📄 Sentiment vs Trader Behavior — Summary

Methodology

This project analyzes the relationship between Bitcoin market sentiment (Fear/Greed) and trader performance on Hyperliquid. The datasets were cleaned by removing duplicates, handling missing values, and standardizing column formats.

Timestamps were converted and aligned at a daily level, and both datasets were merged using a common date field. Key performance metrics such as average profit and loss (PnL), win rate, trade size, and trading frequency were calculated.

To ensure meaningful analysis, trades with zero PnL were removed. Additionally, outliers were filtered in visualizations to improve clarity while retaining full data for calculations.

---

Key Insights

1. **Extreme Greed leads to highest profitability and win rate**

   * Traders achieve maximum average PnL and highest success rates
   * Indicates strong momentum-based trading opportunities

2. **Fear markets outperform normal Greed conditions**

   * Higher win rates and better returns compared to standard Greed phases
   * Suggests disciplined traders benefit from buying during pessimistic conditions

3. **Trader behavior becomes more aggressive during Greed**

   * Increased average trade sizes during Greed phases
   * Reflects higher risk-taking and confidence in bullish markets

---

Strategy Recommendations

1. **Adopt trend-following strategies during Extreme Greed**

   * Increase exposure to capitalize on strong market momentum

2. **Use disciplined dip-buying during Fear phases**

   * Enter positions cautiously with controlled position sizes

3. **Avoid overtrading during normal Greed conditions**

   * Lower performance indicates inefficiency and late market entries
